# CMTO Assignment 4 — Production Plan: "Bayn El Alameen"

> MUB461-O: Concert Touring & Management Operations, Full Sail University. A full production plan with narration script, presentation deck, stage plot, and performance rider for Wahhab's debut headline show.

---

## Show Concept

**"Bayn El Alameen" (Between Two Worlds)** — The stage is split into two visual worlds representing the duality of Wahhab's Egyptian-American identity. Stage left = Cairo (warm amber lighting, Arabic calligraphy projections, traditional textures). Stage right = the West (cool blue/purple tones, urban skyline silhouettes, trap-style energy). As the set progresses, both worlds merge into the DBLU brand's burgundy and gold — symbolizing the artist finding his voice between cultures. It's a narrative arc the audience will feel.

---

## Venue

**El Sawy Culture Wheel** — River Hall, Zamalek, Cairo. Capacity ~400. Sits on the bank of the Nile. Built-in PA, basic lighting, dedicated technical crew. History of hosting independent music and cultural events.

---

## Technical Breakdown

**Stage:** 24×16 ft main stage with 6-ft thrust extension into the audience.

**Lighting:** 32-fixture rig — LED wash lights, moving heads, strobes. Two color palettes (warm amber/gold for Cairo, cool blue/purple for West), dramatic shift to burgundy/gold for the climax.

**Audio:** Line array PA with two 18-inch subwoofers. Separate FOH engineer and monitor engineer. Artist on in-ear mix.

**Video:** Rear-projection screen (full stage width) displaying custom motion graphics — calligraphy, skyline silhouettes, DBLU brand visuals — synced to the setlist via media server.

**Wardrobe:** Opens in a traditional white thobe, transitions to a custom DBLU burgundy bomber jacket for the show's second half — visually marking the cultural fusion.

No pyrotechnics — clean focus on light and sound.

---

## Crew & Equipment

**Venue provides:** House PA, basic lighting, stage manager, 2 stagehands, security.
**DBLU brings:** Lighting designer, DJ, video operator, FOH audio engineer.
**Rented:** LED rear-projection screen + media server, additional moving-head lights.
**Total crew on show day:** 12 people.

## Show Flow (75 minutes)

| Time | Segment |
|---|---|
| 9:00–9:05 | Atmospheric intro — Arabic calligraphy projection, oud sample |
| 9:05–9:25 | Set 1 — "Cairo world" aesthetic, 4 Arabic tracks |
| 9:25–9:30 | Peak moment (lead single) + DJ interlude/transition |
| 9:30–9:55 | Set 2 — "Western world," English/bilingual tracks |
| 9:55–10:05 | Climax — worlds merge, burgundy & gold, hardest-hitting track |
| 10:05–10:15 | 2-song encore |

---

## Performance Rider Summary

**Catering:** Hot meal for 8 (artist, DJ, 6 crew) by 6 PM. Halal-certified, no pork. One hot entree, one vegetarian, salad, fruit, drinks.

**Dressing Room:** One private room for 6 people. Full-length mirror, power outlets, bottled water (12), fresh fruit, assorted soft drinks, hot tea/honey, light snacks.

**Technical Requirements (in addition to house system):** 2 DI boxes (DJ), 1 wireless vocal mic (Shure SM58 or equiv.) + 1 wired backup, 2 monitor wedges, power strips at DJ booth, HDMI input for projection system.

**Additional:** Secure parking for crew van, 10-name guest list (48 hrs advance), merch table with power outlet, Wi-Fi access.

---

## Contingency Plans

1. **Media server failure:** Full video content backed up on 2 separate laptops; video operator can switch to direct HDMI feed within minutes
2. **Cairo traffic:** Artist staying 10 min from venue; all equipment deliveries scheduled early morning
3. **Power fluctuations:** UPS backup for media server and audio console
4. **Bilingual audience:** All stage announcements and signage fully bilingual (Arabic/English)
