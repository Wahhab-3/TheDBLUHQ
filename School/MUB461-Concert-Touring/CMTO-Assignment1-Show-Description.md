## Part 1: Show Description — Mohamed Ramadan at Coachella 2025

On April 13, 2025, Egyptian superstar Mohamed Ramadan became the first Egyptian artist to perform at Coachella, playing a 40-minute set in the Gobi Tent at the Empire Polo Club in Indio, California. He shared the festival bill with Lady Gaga, Ed Sheeran, and Travis Scott.

### Design Elements Analyzed

**Costume & Visual Identity:** Custom haute couture ensemble by Egyptian designer Farida Temraza — a gold chain top, black trousers, and a long black cardigan with a hand-beaded Ankh (ancient Egyptian "Key of Life") symbol. The gold-and-black palette conveyed royalty and power, connecting Ramadan's modern pop identity to thousands of years of Egyptian heritage.

**Cultural Symbolism as Design:** Ramadan raised the Egyptian flag during "Bethlawy," transforming the performance into a declaration of national pride. This moment became one of the most widely shared clips from Coachella 2025 on social media.

**Venue Context:** The Gobi Tent's intimate, enclosed setting amplified Ramadan's call-and-response energy. The tent's lighting capabilities allowed dramatic stage washes complementing the gold tones of his costume, and the enclosed acoustics amplified the bass lines of his Arabic pop tracks.

**Controversy as Design Element:** The outfit sparked massive backlash on Egyptian social media. Critics compared the look to traditional women's dance costumes. Sayed Mahmoud, advisor to the Federation of Three Artistic Syndicates, announced a formal investigation into whether the costume was appropriate for representing Egypt abroad. The controversy itself became an unintended design element — raising questions about artistic freedom, cultural responsibility, and who defines appropriate Egyptian representation.

**Long-term Impact:** In February 2026, Ramadan signed with WME (William Morris Endeavor), directly resulting from the visibility his Coachella performance generated.

## Part 2: Current Events in the Live Music Industry

### Legal: United States v. Live Nation Entertainment

On March 3, 2026, the U.S. DOJ brought antitrust charges against Live Nation and Ticketmaster in the Southern District of New York. The lawsuit alleges Live Nation monopolized the live concert industry by controlling ticketing, concert booking, venue operations, and promotions simultaneously — controlling approximately 80% of major concert venue ticketing. Evidence includes the 2022 Taylor Swift Eras Tour ticketing disaster. Bloomberg reported Live Nation CEO Michael Rapino threatened to withhold concerts from Barclays Center when the arena attempted switching from Ticketmaster to SeatGeek. The trial is expected to last six weeks and could force Live Nation to divest Ticketmaster entirely.

**Wahhab's take:** The monopolistic control hurts both fans (inflated prices, no competition) and artists (limited venue choices, forced into the Ticketmaster ecosystem). This trial is shining a necessary light on how corporate consolidation has fundamentally altered the live music experience.

### Ethical: Dynamic Pricing / Cultural Representation

The research brief explored two ethical angles: Ticketmaster's dynamic pricing (where Oasis reunion tickets surged from £135 to £355) and Mohamed Ramadan's cultural representation controversy at Coachella — whether artistic freedom or cultural responsibility should prevail when representing one's nation on a global stage.

### Public Safety: Fenway Park Stage Safety Failure

In May 2025, multiple concerts (Shakira, Jason Aldean, Brooks & Dunn) were canceled at Fenway Park after staging failed during rehearsal. Boston officials found the staging was structurally unsound — a direct public safety failure at a major venue.
