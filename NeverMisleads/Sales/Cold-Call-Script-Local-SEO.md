# Cold Call Script — Local SEO

## Opening

**"Hi, is this [Owner Name]?"**
→ Wait for confirmation

**"My name is [Your Name], I'm calling from NeverMisleads. I noticed [Business Name] in [City] and I had a quick question — do you mind?"**

*Tone: Friendly, conversational, brief. You're not imposing; you're just asking for a moment.*

---

## The Hook

**"When someone in [City] searches Google for [their service], does your business show up in the top 3 on the map?"**

Most will say **"No"** or **"I'm not sure."**

→ **"That's exactly why I'm calling. We help [service type] businesses like yours get found in that top 3 spot. When you appear there, you get qualified leads without paying Google for ads every single time."**

This frames the problem they likely already know they have and positions you as the solution.

---

## Qualify

Ask 3 quick questions to gauge fit:

**Question 1:** "How are you currently getting new customers — word of mouth, Google, referrals, something else?"

*Listen.* This tells you their current strategy and awareness level.

**Question 2:** "Have you worked with an SEO person or agency before?"

*Listen.* If yes, ask why it ended (might reveal pain points). If no, they might be more open but less educated on value.

**Question 3:** "If I could show you exactly where you stand on Google and what the next 30 days could look like, would it be worth 15 minutes this week?"

*This is the commitment question.* If they say yes, you have a lead. If no, move on gracefully.

---

## 3 Objection Handlers

### Objection 1: "I'm not interested"
**Your response:**
"I totally get it — most business owners are busy. I'm not trying to sell you anything right now. I just wanted to send over a quick report showing where [Business Name] shows up on Google compared to your top competitors in [City]. Takes 2 minutes to look at. Fair?"

*Then:* "What's the best email to send that to?"

→ You've lowered the barrier. They might look at it. If they do, follow up in 24 hours.

---

### Objection 2: "We already have someone doing this for us"
**Your response:**
"I hear that. Quick question though — when someone searches '[service type] near me' in [City], are you showing up in the top 3 on the local map pack? Because that's where most of the calls come from."

*Pause. Let them answer.*

If **yes:** "That's great. Out of curiosity, how many leads are you getting per week from that spot?"

If **no:** "See, that's what we focus on. A lot of agencies miss that. The map pack is where the money is. Worth a quick 15-minute conversation just to see if there's room for improvement?"

→ This plants doubt about their current provider and positions you as the expert.

---

### Objection 3: "How much does it cost?"
**Your response:**
"Great question. But here's the thing — I can't give you a real number until I know exactly where you stand and what we'd need to do. Some businesses need light optimization; some need more work. What I CAN do is show you the specifics and give you a real quote after our 15-minute call. Sound fair?"

*Then:* "Let's book a quick call for [Day/Time]. I'll pull a full report on your visibility before we talk, so you'll have real data in front of you."

→ You've made the case that price depends on the situation, and you've moved them toward booking.

---

## Close

If they're interested after qualifying:

**"Perfect. Let's lock in 15 minutes this week. What works better for you — [Day A at Time A] or [Day B at Time B]?"**

*Give two specific options.* Don't ask "When are you free?" — that creates decision paralysis.

Once they pick:

**"Great. I'll send you a calendar invite and a quick report before we talk so you have the data in front of you. Looking forward to it."**

---

## After the Call

### If Interested
- **Within 5 minutes:** Send calendar invite + report as promised
- Log in CRM with next steps and proposed project scope
- Prepare a basic quote based on what you discussed

### If They Said "Call Me Back"
- **Note the date** in your CRM
- Set a calendar reminder for the morning of
- Call at a slightly earlier time than discussed (9 AM instead of 10 AM) to catch them
- Have the report ready before you call

### If Not Interested / Wrong Fit
- **Move on.** No follow-up unless they specifically asked you to.
- Log "Not interested" and reason (too small, already has solution, not motivated, etc.)
- This data helps you refine your target list

### If Left Voicemail
Keep it short (under 30 seconds):

"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from NeverMisleads. I was calling about getting [Business] showing up in the top 3 on Google maps in [City]. If that's something you'd be open to, give me a call back at [Your Number]. Looking forward to it."

→ **Don't** ask them to call back unless they've shown interest. Better to catch them live.

---

## Key Principles

1. **The hook works.** Most service businesses know they need to show up on Google. You're validating their problem.
2. **Three questions = qualification.** Don't spend 10 minutes on the phone with someone who isn't a fit.
3. **Objections are natural.** They're not saying no to YOU; they're protecting their time. Respect that.
4. **The calendar invite is your commitment.** Once you send it, you've converted them. Follow through.
5. **Speed matters.** If they're interested, send the report and lock the call within 2 hours. Momentum dies fast.

---

## Daily Targets

- **10 calls per day** (aim for 8-10 live conversations; 2-3 will be voicemail)
- **2-3 qualified conversations** (they answer the three questions and say yes to 15 min)
- **1-2 calendar invites sent** (follow-up same day)
- **Weekly: 50-70 calls, 10-15 qualified, 5-10 meetings booked**
