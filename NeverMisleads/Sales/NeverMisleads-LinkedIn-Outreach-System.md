# NeverMisleads LinkedIn Outreach System

## Daily Routine (30-45 min/day)

### Morning Block (15 min)
- Log in to Sales Navigator
- Execute searches and send 10 connection requests
- Review acceptances from past 3 days
- Send Message #1 to new connections (warm, not salesy)
- Review and respond to existing conversations

### Evening Block (15 min)
- Review daily metrics
- Send follow-up messages (#2, #3, #4, #5 as applicable)
- Log outcomes in tracking table
- Plan next day's outreach targets

## 5 Message Sequence

### Connection Request Note (300 char max)

**Template A:**
"Hi [Name], I work with local service businesses like yours on their online visibility. Would love to connect and share a quick insight about [City]."

**Template B:**
"[Name], noticed [Business] and your focus on [specific thing from their profile]. I help businesses in your space grow locally. Let's connect?"

**Template C:**
"Hey [Name], been following local business growth in [City] and [Business] stood out. Brief question — can I add you?"

---

### Message #1 (Day after acceptance)
*Warm, not salesy. Build rapport.*

"Hey [Name], thanks for connecting. I work with businesses like yours on their Google visibility in [City]. When you get a chance, would be happy to share a quick insight. No pressure — just wanted to say thanks for the add."

---

### Message #2 (If positive response)
*The Hook — build before you pay.*

"Glad you're open to it. Quick context: I help [service type] businesses appear in Google's local map when people search for [service] in [city]. Most of our clients get 3-5 qualified leads per week once they're set up properly.

Before we talk, let me ask — when someone searches '[service type] near me' in [city], does your business show up in the top 3 on the map? Or is there room to improve?"

---

### Message #3 (If yes to opportunity)
*Lock it in with Google Meet.*

"Perfect. Let's hop on a quick 15-minute call so I can show you exactly where you stand and what the next 30 days could look like for [Business].

What works better — [Day/Time A] or [Day/Time B] this week? I'll send you a Google Meet link."

---

### Message #4 (Gentle nudge, Day 3-4)
*If they haven't booked.*

"[Name], didn't want to let this slip — the businesses we work with move fast on this. Just sent you two time slots for this week. Let me know which works."

---

### Message #5 (Breakup, Day 7-10)
*If no response to Messages #3-#4.*

"[Name], I'll keep this brief. Looks like timing might not be right, and that's totally okay. If things change and you want to explore growing your Google visibility, I'm here. Best of luck with [Business]."

## Content Strategy

1. **Local SEO Tip:** "3 quick wins to show up in Google Maps for [service]"
2. **Case Study Teaser:** "How we got [Business] 5 leads in week 1 on Google"
3. **Industry Insight:** Pain point for service businesses (e.g., "90% of [service] businesses don't appear in top 3 local results")
4. **Authority Build:** Industry stat or trend in local service business space
5. **Engagement Play:** "What's the #1 complaint from your customers about your online presence?" — let comments drive engagement

### Posting Rules
- Post 1x per week (Tuesday-Thursday, 8 AM your timezone)
- Keep it relevant to local service business owners
- Always tie back to the value you provide
- Encourage comments with a question at the end
- Tag relevant industries or locations when appropriate

## Rules to Live By

1. **Lead with value, never with a pitch.** The first message is about building rapport and relevance, not selling.
2. **Personalize every message.** Generic templates fail. Reference something from their profile, company, or recent activity.
3. **Respect the sequence.** Don't skip steps. The 5-message sequence works because each message builds on the last.
4. **Speed matters.** Send Message #1 within 24 hours of acceptance. The sooner you engage, the warmer the conversation.
5. **Always give them an out.** "No pressure," "totally understand if timing's not right," "just wanted to say thanks." People respond to respect.
6. **Log everything.** Your tracking table is your sales intelligence. Use it to spot patterns and improve.
7. **Test and iterate.** A/B test your connection request templates. Track what works and lean into it.
8. **Protect your reputation.** LinkedIn is a long game. One spammy approach burns trust. Stay genuine.

## LinkedIn Limits
- **Under 100 connection requests per week**
- Best practice: 10-15 per day, spread across multiple times
- Avoid bulk actions; space out sends
- Mix outreach with genuine engagement (commenting, sharing relevant content)
