# Facebook Lead Ad Campaign — Complete Strategy & Execution Plan

## 1. Campaign Overview

### Objective
Generate qualified leads from Facebook and Instagram specifically targeting service business owners. Convert these leads into discovery calls and eventually paying customers.

### Budget & Timeline
- **Starting budget:** $2/day ($60/month) for Month 1
- **Scale to:** $5-10/day ($150-300/month) Month 2-3
- **Full scale:** $200-400/month by Month 3 (if ROAS is strong)

### Performance Targets
- **Cost Per Lead (CPL):** $3-6 per lead (aggressive $2-4, realistic $5-8)
- **Form Completion Rate:** 10-20% of clickers
- **Lead Quality:** 30-50% actually book a call (rest go to nurture)
- **Booking Rate:** 20-30% of leads book a discovery call
- **Close Rate:** 25-35% of discovery calls close to client
- **Overall ROAS:** Minimum 3:1 (spend $60, get $180+ in client value)

### Volume Projection
- **Month 1:** $60 budget → 10-20 leads → 2-4 booked calls → 0-2 closures (ramping month)
- **Month 2:** $200 budget → 30-40 leads → 6-12 booked calls → 2-5 closures
- **Month 3:** $400 budget → 60-100 leads → 15-30 booked calls → 4-10 closures

At 30% close rate from booked calls, even conservative numbers yield 2-5 new clients/month from paid ads alone.

### Funnel Flow
```

       ↓

       ↓

```

## 2. Targeting Strategy

### Audience 1 (Primary): Service Business Owners

**Demographics:**
- Location: United States (all states, phases to focus regions later)
- Age: 28-65 (peak age 35-55)
- Gender: All (skew toward male for trades, but don't exclude female)

**Job Title Targeting** (use Facebook "Job Title" field):
- Owner
- Founder
- CEO
- President
- Operator
- Manager
- General Contractor

**Industry Targeting** (use Facebook "Industry" field):
- Plumbing
- HVAC / Heating & Cooling
- Electrical Contractors
- Home Repair
- Construction
- Roofing
- Landscaping
- Cleaning Services
- Handyman Services
- Concrete Services
- Tree Services
- Pest Control
- Water Damage Restoration

**Company Size:**
- 1-50 employees (focus on 1-20 for Phase 1)

**Income Level (Optional):**
- $75K+ household income (filters out lower-intent prospects)

**Exclusions:**
- Marketing agencies (we're not selling to them)
- People who already visited website and booked
- People who already follow our page
- People under 28 or over 65 (different buying behavior)

**Estimated Reach:** 2-3M people in US matching these criteria

### Audience 2 (Phase 2, Week 3+): 1% Lookalike from Lead Form Completions

Once we have 50-100 people complete our lead form, create a 1% lookalike audience based on their characteristics. Facebook will find similar users.

- **Timing:** Start Week 3-4 when we have first 50 leads
- **Budget:** $0.50-1/day initially
- **Performance:** Expect 30-50% better CPL than cold audience

### Audience 3 (Phase 2, Week 3+): Retargeting — Website Visitors

Retarget people who took specific actions but didn't convert:

**Retargeting Segment A: Website Visitor, No Form**
- People who visited website but didn't complete lead form
- Message: "See what results look like" (case study or demo video)
- Frequency: 3x per week

**Retargeting Segment B: Form Opener, No Submit**
- People who opened the form but abandoned before completing
- Message: "Most businesses are shocked by these numbers" (calculator result teaser)
- Frequency: 2x per week

**Retargeting Segment C: Booked Call, Before Call**
- People who booked but appointment is >24 hours away
- Message: "Get excited — here's what we'll show you" (preview of offer)
- Frequency: 1x per week

**Retargeting Segment D: No Action, Sustained Interest**
- People who engaged with content (comment, share, video view 75%+) but haven't visited website
- Message: "See your custom website demo for free" (direct offer)
- Frequency: 2x per week

### Exclusions (Across All Audiences)

- Existing leads (not form in last 6 months)
- Existing customers
- Marketing agencies
- Software companies
- Job seekers or people in job-seeking categories
- Small audience blockers (not US-based, clearly bot patterns)

## 3. Campaign Structure

### Two Ad Sets (Separate Audiences, Separate Messaging)

The Facebook campaign will have **two distinct ad sets**, each targeting a different pain point with different creative:

**Ad Set A: "Google Visibility & Reviews"**
- Audience: Business owners worried about Google visibility
- Angle: You're invisible on Google. Your competitors are found. Here's why.
- Lead Magnet: Google Visibility Scorecard
- Creative themes: Comparison (you vs competitors), Statistics, Review gap

**Ad Set B: "AI Receptionist & Missed Calls"**
- Audience: Business owners frustrated with missed calls
- Angle: You're leaving money on the table every time you miss a call. Here's the solution.
- Lead Magnet: Missed Call Calculator (implied in ad)
- Creative themes: The cost of missing calls, 24/7 availability, Appointment booking

**Rationale:** Different owners care about different problems. By splitting audiences and messaging, we achieve higher relevance scores and better conversion rates. We'll test both, identify winner, and scale.

## 6. Lead Form Setup (Detailed Specifications)

### Form Type: "Higher Intent" (Not "Standard Lead Form")

Higher Intent forms are more customizable and have built-in qualification flow. This is different from Standard Lead Forms which are more generic.

### Pre-filled Fields (Reduce Friction)

These fields auto-populate if the user is logged into Facebook (reduce clicks):
- Name (first, last)
- Phone number
- Email address

User sees form and it's already halfway filled. They just confirm and add answers.

### Form Intro Screen

**Headline:**
"See Your Free Google Visibility Scorecard"

OR

"See If Maya Is Right for Your Business"

(Vary by ad set)

**Description:**
"Answer 3 quick questions (60 seconds). We'll generate your personalized report and schedule a time to chat."

OR (for receptionist focus)

"Answer 4 quick questions about your business (90 seconds). We'll show you how many calls you're probably missing and what it costs you annually."

**Visual:**
Progress bar showing "Question 1 of 4" (visual reassurance it's quick)

### Questions

**Question 1: Business Type (Qualification Gate)**

Type: Multiple choice (single select)

"What's your primary service?"

**Options:**
- Plumbing
- HVAC / Heating & Cooling
- Electrical
- Roofing
- Landscaping
- Cleaning Services
- General Contracting
- Other Home Services

**Logic:**
- Show all options
- If "Other" selected, follow-up question: "Tell us more" (text input)
- **Disqualifier note:** If prospect selects "Marketing Agency" or "Software Company," end form with "We work directly with service business owners. Check back later!" and don't submit lead.

---

**Question 2: Google Review Count (Interest Level)**

Type: Multiple choice (single select)

"How many reviews do you currently have on Google?"

**Options:**
1. 0-10 reviews (HIGH PRIORITY — likely invisible, big pain)
2. 11-30 reviews (MEDIUM PRIORITY — some reviews, wants more)
3. 31-75 reviews (MEDIUM PRIORITY — decent, wants to grow)
4. 75+ reviews (LOWER PRIORITY — already has social proof, maybe not as desperate)
5. Not sure / Don't track (MEDIUM PRIORITY — doesn't track metrics, might need education)

**Lead Scoring Note:**
- 0-10 reviews = +3 points (very high priority)
- 11-30 = +2 points
- 31-75 = +1 point
- 75+ = 0 points
- Not sure = +1 point

## 9. Maya's Updated Role: Warm Lead Follow-Up (Not Cold Calling)

### Difference from Cold Calling

**Cold Calling (Current):**
- Wahhab calls random business owners from a list
- They have no prior context or interest signal
- Conversion rate: 25-30% to book demo
- Takes energy, requires good pitch

**Warm Follow-Up (New):**
- Maya calls people who ALREADY submitted a form
- They've already raised their hand and answered qualification questions
- They've said "I'm interested in [website / AI receptionist]"
- Conversion rate: 40-60% to book demo (much higher intent)
- Less energy required, simpler script

### Maya's Script (Warm Follow-Up)

**Opening (2 sec):**
"Hi [Name], this is Maya. I'm an AI assistant with [[NeverMisleads]]. I see you just filled out our form about getting found on Google. Is this still a good time?"

**If Yes, Continue:**

"Great. I have a couple of quick questions about what you told us.

You mentioned your biggest challenge is [PAIN POINT THEY SELECTED]. Is that still accurate?

[Listen for 10-15 seconds]

Okay. And when are you looking to make a change — was it this month or a bit more down the road?

[Listen for 10-15 seconds]

Perfect. Here's what I'd suggest: [[Wahhab]], our founder, builds a free custom website demo for local service businesses so you can see exactly what you'd get before you commit. It usually takes 3-5 days to build.

Would that be interesting to you?"

**If Interested, Continue:**

"Great. Let me get you scheduled. What day and time work best this week for a 15-minute call with [[Wahhab]]? He'll ask a couple questions, then build your demo."

[Open Calendly link, book time]

"Perfect, I've sent you the calendar link. You'll get a confirmation email. [[Wahhab]] will call you then. Looking forward to showing you what's possible."

**If Not Interested:**

"No problem. I get it, not the right time. Can I follow up with you in 30 days to see if anything's changed?"

[If yes, add to email nurture. If no, remove and note "opted out."]

**If Not a Good Time:**

"Totally understand. When's a better time this week?"

[Find another time. If too hard to schedule, suggest: "Let me send you a link where you can pick your own time. How's that?"]

## 14. Testing Plan & Scaling Rules

### Week 1-2: All 8 ads running equally ($0.25/day each)

**Goal:** Identify 2-3 clear winners

**Metrics to track:**
- CTR: Which ads get clicks?
- Lead quality: Which lead forms convert?
- Form completion time: Which questions cause drop-off?

**Decision rules:**
- If one ad has 2x better CTR than average → **Keep it**
- If one ad has 3x worse CTR → **Kill it**
- If form drop-off happens on a question → **Remove or rephrase question**

---

### Week 3: Eliminate & Double Down

**Action:** Kill bottom 2-4 ads. Double budget on top performers.

**New allocation:**
- Winners: $0.50-1/day each (depends on how many won)
- Losers: Pause completely
- Budget stays $2/day total

**New learnings to test:**
- Add lookalike audience ($0.25/day)
- Start retargeting website visitors ($0.25/day)

---

### Month 2: Scale winners, test new creative

**Winners** (from Month 1):
- Scale to $2-3/day each
- Keep same targeting/audience

**New creative tests:**
- Create 2-3 new variations of winning concepts
- Run at $0.50/day each
- If new variation beats winner → replace winner

**Audience expansion:**
- Lookalike from lead completions: $1-2/day
- New audience based on different job titles
- Retargeting: $2-3/day (multi-segment)

**Total budget: $200-300/month**

---

### Month 3: Aggressive scaling, continuous testing

**Scaling rules (if ROAS is strong):**
- If CPL < $3: Double budget allocation
- If CPL $3-6: Maintain or increase 20%
- If CPL > $8: Cut budget 50%, investigate
- If ROAS > 5x: Scale as much as Facebook allows
- If ROAS < 2x: Pause, retest with new creative

**Testing plan:**
- Continue monthly creative tests
- Test new audience segments quarterly
- A/B test landing pages (form vs direct booking link)
- Test different offer angles (free demo vs calculator vs scorecard)
