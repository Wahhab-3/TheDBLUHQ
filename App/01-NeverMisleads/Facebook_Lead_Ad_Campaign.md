# NeverMisleads — Facebook Lead Ad Campaign

## Campaign Overview

**Objective:** Lead Generation (Facebook Lead Ads)
**Starting Budget:** $2/day ($60/month — same as Apollo was costing)
**Goal:** 10-20 qualified leads/month at $3-6 CPL
**Funnel:** Ad → Survey Form → Auto-Book Discovery Call → Maya Follow-Up (warm leads who don't book)

---

## Targeting Strategy

### Audience 1: Service Business Owners (Primary)
- **Job Titles:** Owner, Founder, CEO, President, General Manager, Operations Manager
- **Industries:** Home Services, Plumbing, HVAC, Electrical, Roofing, Landscaping, Pest Control, Cleaning Services
- **Company Size:** 1-50 employees
- **Location:** United States (start broad, narrow after data)
- **Age:** 28-65
- **Behaviors:** Small business owners, Business page admins

### Audience 2: Lookalike (Phase 2 — after 50+ leads)
- 1% lookalike based on lead form completions
- Layer with Home Services interest targeting

### Audience 3: Retargeting (Phase 2)
- Website visitors (Apollo pixel is already installed)
- Video viewers (75%+ watch time)
- Lead form openers who didn't submit

### Exclusions
- Exclude existing leads/customers (upload list when available)
- Exclude marketing agencies and freelancers
- Exclude job seekers

**Question 1: What type of service business do you run?**
(Multiple Choice)
- Plumbing

- Electrical
- Landscaping / Lawn Care
- Cleaning Services
- Other Home Services
- Not a service business ← *disqualifier*

(Multiple Choice)

- 51-100 reviews ← *good candidate*

- I don't know ← *needs education, still qualified*

(Multiple Choice)

**Question 4: When would you like to get started?**
(Multiple Choice)
- Immediately — I'm losing business right now ← *hot lead*
- Within the next 2 weeks ← *warm lead*
- Within the next month ← *warm lead*
- Just exploring options ← *cool lead, nurture*

## Maya's Updated Role: Warm Lead Follow-Up

Maya's new job is NOT cold calling. She calls people who already:
1. Saw the ad
2. Filled out the survey
3. Showed interest but didn't book a call

### Maya's Follow-Up Script Outline

**Opening:** "Hi [Name], this is Maya from NeverMisleads! You filled out a quick survey about getting more visibility on Google for your [business type] business. I'm calling to see if you'd like to book a free 15-minute discovery call with our team."

**If interested:** Book via Google Calendar API, confirm time, send SMS confirmation.

**If not ready:** "No problem! Would it be okay if we checked back in a couple weeks? In the meantime, I can text you a free guide on how to get more Google reviews."

**If they have questions:** Answer basic questions about NeverMisleads services, then push to book the call for detailed discussion.

## Budget & Expected Performance

### Starting Budget: $2/day ($60/month)

**Conservative Estimates (local service niche on Facebook):**

| Metric | Conservative | Optimistic |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| CPM (cost per 1,000 impressions) | $15-25 | $10-15 |
| CTR (click-through rate) | 1.5% | 3% |
| Form completion rate | 15% | 30% |
| CPL (cost per lead) | $5-8 | $2-4 |
| Leads per month | 8-12 | 15-30 |
| Booking rate (from leads) | 20-30% | 30-50% |
| Discovery calls/month | 2-4 | 5-15 |
| Close rate | 25-40% | 40-60% |
| **New clients/month** | **1-2** | **2-6** |

**At $500/mo per client, even 1 new client = 8x ROAS on $60 ad spend.**

### Scaling Plan
- **Week 1-2:** Run all 4 ad variations at $0.50/day each
- **Week 3:** Kill the 2 worst performers, reallocate budget to winners
- **Week 4:** Double budget on the winning ad ($4/day total)
- **Month 2:** If CPL is good, scale to $5-10/day ($150-300/month)
- **Month 3+:** Add retargeting audiences, build lookalike from leads

## Integration: Lead Form → Auto-Book → Maya

**Recommended automation (n8n or Zapier):**

1. **New lead submits form** → Webhook fires
2. **Add to Google Sheet** (lead tracking)
3. **Score the lead** (based on survey answers)
4. **If lead books via Thank You screen:** Confirm booking, send SMS via Twilio
5. **If lead does NOT book within 2 hours (hot) or 24 hours (warm):**
   - Trigger Maya (VAPI) to call them
   - Pass context: name, business type, challenge, score
6. **After Maya's call:**
   - If booked → Add to calendar, send confirmation
   - If nurture → Add to email/SMS drip sequence

2. **Video Creative Strategy: Avatar vs. Wahhab A/B Test**

   **Option A — Wahhab (authentic, founder-led)**
   - 30-second talking head filmed on phone
   - Raw, real, no polish — this performs better on Facebook than overproduced content
   - Script: "If you're a plumber, HVAC tech, or roofer and you're tired of your competitors getting all the Google calls..."
   - Best for: Trust-building, straight talk angle, brand credibility
   - Advantage: When they meet you on the discovery call, it's consistent

   **Option B — AI Avatar "Maya" (attention-grabbing)**
   - Use an AI avatar tool (HeyGen, Synthesia, D-ID) to create a realistic female presenter
   - IMPORTANT: Present her honestly as "Maya, our AI assistant" — this is on-brand (you sell AI receptionists!) and avoids any misleading perception
   - Script: Same copy as Wahhab version but delivered by the avatar
   - Best for: Stopping the scroll, higher CTR, demo of your actual AI product
   - Advantage: Living proof that your AI tech looks and sounds real

   **Option C — Combo (recommended for Receptionist ads)**
   - Maya (avatar) delivers the hook: "Hi, I'm Maya — I'm an AI receptionist built for service businesses"
   - Cut to Wahhab: "I built Maya because I was tired of seeing good businesses lose jobs to missed calls"
   - This shows the product AND the founder in one video
   - Best for: Receptionist ad set specifically

   **Testing Plan:**
   - Week 1-2: Run both Wahhab and Maya versions of the top-performing static ad
   - Compare: CTR (who stops the scroll), CPL (who converts), and booking rate (who becomes a real lead)
   - The winner gets the budget. If they're close, keep both.

   **Brand Alignment Note:**
   Your company is called NeverMisleads. Using an AI avatar IS fine as long as you're transparent that she's AI. In fact, for the receptionist ad set, Maya the avatar IS the product demo. That's not misleading — that's a live preview. The key is never pretending she's a real human employee.
