## THE ELEVATOR PITCH (15-30 seconds)

When someone asks "so what do you do?"

> "I run a branding and marketing agency called NeverMisleads. I work with local businesses — if your website is outdated or you don't have one, I'll build it for you before we even have our first meeting. You show up to the call and your site's already there. From there I handle everything — your online visibility, your branding, and I automate the stuff that's eating up your time, like missed calls, follow-ups, appointment booking. Basically I make your business look professional and run smoother without you hiring anyone."

If they look interested, land the hook:

> "The cool thing is there's no deposit, no guessing — you see exactly what you're getting before you spend a dollar."

Then stop talking. Let them respond.

## WHEN THEY COME UP TO YOU AFTER

**Opening — always ask about them first:**

> "Yeah for sure — what kind of business do you run?"

Let them talk. Then:

> "Nice. So here's what I'd do — I'd take a look at your brand, your Instagram, your current site if you have one, and I'd build you something that actually represents what you do. Most of my clients see it on our first Google Meet and they're like 'wait, this is already done?' That's the whole point — I don't want you guessing what you're paying for."

**Then transition to automation (this is the money part):**

> "And honestly, the website is just the foundation. What really changes things for my clients is when I automate their operations. Like, how are you handling calls right now when you're busy or after hours?"

*(Let them answer — they'll almost always say they miss calls or it goes to voicemail)*

> "Yeah, that's what I hear from everyone. So what I do is set up an AI system that answers your calls, captures the lead info, books appointments, and sends you a notification — all without you touching anything. And that's just one example. I automate follow-up emails, review requests, appointment reminders, lead routing — whatever's eating up your time. The whole point is your business runs even when you're not sitting there managing it."

Never leave a conversation without this:

> "Hey, let me grab your number — I'll pull up your business tonight and shoot you a text with what I'd do. No commitment, I just want to show you what's possible."

**Or if they seem really interested:**

## OBJECTION HANDLING

**"I already have a website"**
> "That's great — when was the last time you updated it? Most businesses I work with have a site, but it's not doing anything for them. It's not showing up when people search, it's not converting visitors into customers. I can take a look and tell you exactly what's working and what's not — free of charge."

**"I'll think about it"**
> "Totally fair. Here's what I'll do — let me grab your business name and I'll put something together for you anyway. No charge, no commitment. If you like what you see, we'll talk. If not, no hard feelings. Sound fair?"

**"I have a friend/nephew who does websites"**
> "I hear that a lot. And look, a website is just the starting point for me. What I really do is make sure people actually find you online and then automate the stuff that's costing you time — missed calls, manual follow-ups, scheduling. That's the part most people can't get from a friend with Squarespace."

**"I don't need marketing, I get all my business from referrals"**
> "That's amazing, and referrals are the best kind of business. But here's the thing — when someone gets referred to you, the first thing they do is Google you. If they don't find a professional website and good reviews, they second-guess the referral. I make sure that when people look you up, they're impressed before they even call."

**"What makes you different from other agencies?"**
> "Two things. One — I build your website before you pay me anything. You see it on our first call, fully built around your brand. No other agency does that. Two — I don't just build a website and disappear. I handle your visibility, your automation, your entire digital operations. Most agencies hand you a site and invoice you. I make sure your phone rings and your calendar fills up."

**"I can't afford it right now"**
> "I hear you. That's actually why I made the Starter at $149 with no setup fee — it's designed to be a no-brainer entry point. And honestly, once the website starts bringing in even one or two extra customers a month, it more than pays for itself. Can you afford to keep missing those customers?"

## NETWORKING EVENT MINDSET

**Before you walk in:**
- You're not selling. You're solving problems. Every person there has a business that could look better online and run smoother.
- Your goal is CONVERSATIONS, not closes. Get 10-15 phone numbers and business names. That's a win.
- You close on the Google Meet, not at the event. The event is for planting seeds.

**During the event:**
- Ask questions more than you talk. "What do you do? How do you get your customers? What's your biggest headache right now?"
- When they mention ANYTHING related to online presence, missed calls, being too busy, doing everything themselves — that's your opening.
- Don't pitch unless they ask. Just listen, relate, and offer to help.

**After the event:**
- Text every contact within 2 hours: "Hey [Name], great meeting you tonight. I'm going to pull up [business name] and put something together for you. I'll send it over tomorrow."
- Build their site that night or next morning.
- Schedule the Google Meet for within 48 hours. Speed is everything.
