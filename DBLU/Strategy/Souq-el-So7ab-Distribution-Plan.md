## Five Lifestyle Categories

Based on Karim's profile, these five lifestyle categories define his world and guide the marketing, retail, and promotional strategy.

**1. Streetwear Culture** — Karim expresses identity through fashion: oversized hoodies, graphic tees, local Egyptian streetwear brands. Aligns with Wahhab's merch drops and the visual identity of the fanbase.

**2. Football Culture** — Central to Egyptian youth identity. Opens cross-promotional opportunities through football-themed content and events.

**3. Ahwa (Cafe) Culture** — Spending evenings at local cafes socializing is a cornerstone of Egyptian youth life. Ahwas are where music spreads organically through conversation and shared playlists — a grassroots discovery channel.

**4. Gaming Lounge / Cyber Cafe Culture** — PlayStation rental lounges and cyber cafes are social hubs where young Egyptians gather, listen to music, and spend time. Non-traditional retail and promotional channel.

**5. Fitness Culture** — Going to the gym and maintaining a fashionable athletic look. Intersects with streetwear and social media — another touchpoint for brand visibility.

## Digital Distributor — Too Lost

Too Lost was selected based on its ability to deliver music globally while allowing targeted marketing focused on Egypt and the MENA region. Supports independent artists with transparent royalty splits and flexible release management.

**Platforms Included:** Spotify, Apple Music, Anghami, YouTube Music, Amazon Music, TikTok Audio Library, Instagram Reels Audio Library.

**Rationale:** Too Lost includes Anghami (critical for MENA audiences), supports TikTok and Instagram audio libraries (primary discovery channels for Karim's demographic), and aligns with Wahhab's goal of building the DBLU brand independently while maintaining full ownership of masters and publishing.

## Retailer Sheet

Six retailers selected based on alignment with Karim's lifestyle, the artist's brand identity, and grassroots exposure in Cairo.

### Mom & Pop Record Stores

**1. Sherry's** — Zamalek, Cairo. Bahgat Ali St., Masri Towers, Ground Floor. Phone: 0122 312 7208. Originally an antique/collectibles shop that converted to a record store in 2010 due to rising vinyl demand. Impressive Arabic collection alongside jazz, rock, classical, and rare Greek, Armenian, Turkish, and Eastern European 45s. 5,000+ record collection. Promotion: QR code stickers near the Arabic music section, branded stickers/posters for in-store display, small listening session or meet-and-greet.

**2. Mazzika Zaman** — Zamalek, Cairo. Bahgat Ali St., Masri Towers, 3rd Floor. Phone: 0122 768 2049. The oldest remaining record store in Egypt, opened in 1981. 25,000+ records spanning Arabic music, rock, jazz, blues, soul, funk, R&B, folk, and country. Also sells gramophones, tape recorders, vintage posters. Promotion: Display posters alongside vintage Arabic music section, branded QR code cards at the counter, collaborate on a curated "New Egyptian Hip-Hop" listening station.

### Local Lifestyle Retailers

**3. Locally** — Cairo (multiple locations: District 5, CityStars, ParkMall). instagram.com/locally__eg. 129K on Instagram. The first and largest Egyptian brands boutique — curates and sells independent Egyptian fashion brands. Perfect retail partner for Wahhab's merch drops. Promotion: Consign DBLU branded tees and bucket hats, QR code stickers on shopping bags, co-branded Instagram Reels.

**4. The Bay Boutique** — Sahel (North Coast), Egypt. tiktok.com/@the.bay.boutique. The first local brand showroom in Sahel. Hosts seasonal bazaars and showcases independent Egyptian brands during summer season — exactly when Karim and his peers vacation at Amwaj/North Coast. Promotion: Pop-up merch display during Bay Bazaar summer events, branded stickers and posters.
