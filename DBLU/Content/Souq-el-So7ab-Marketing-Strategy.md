# Souq el So7ab — IMC Strategy

> Full Integrated Marketing Communications strategy for [[Wahhab]]'s "Souq el So7ab" single release (June 6, 2026). Full Sail University coursework — Music Business Marketing, Prof. Elizabeth Eneff.

---

## Marketing Objectives

1. **Grow social media following to 5,000+** across Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube within four months of campaign launch.
2. **Achieve 8% average engagement rate** on short-form content within six months, prioritizing saves, shares, and comments over passive views.
3. **Convert 10–15 highly engaged community members** into merchandise purchasers within three months of the initial POD merch drop.
4. **Build an owned audience** by collecting 500+ email sign-ups through the official website and pre-save campaign within six months.
5. **Establish a self-sustaining street team presence** across key locations in Egypt, generating measurable word-of-mouth growth through a gamified competition from Week 3 through release day and beyond.

---

## Marketing Tactics Overview

This IMC strategy combines push and pull promotional techniques. Pull strategies center on organic short-form digital content across TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts. Push strategies include direct marketing and guerrilla marketing through street teams in Egypt.

The integrated approach builds a movement around cultural identity and community belonging rather than traditional mass advertising. Every touchpoint — from a TikTok to a sticker on a laptop to a street team member's pitch — reinforces the same narrative: Wahhab is the voice of modern Arab youth, and DBLU is the emblem of that movement.

---

## Advertising

Organic short-form content over traditional paid advertising. Digital commercials take the form of visually driven short-form videos designed for social media — cultural relevance, storytelling, and visual identity to encourage organic discovery and sharing. Paid advertising used selectively: boosting only the highest-performing organic content to extend reach without disrupting audience trust.

Younger audiences in Egypt and the Arab diaspora are increasingly resistant to traditional paid ads and more likely to engage with content that feels authentic and community-driven. Most promotional budget allocated toward on-the-ground marketing initiatives in Egypt.

---

## Content Strategy and Batching

Structured monthly batching schedule to maintain consistency without burnout. Two content types produced each month:

**Visual Fragments (Animated):** Produced in the first week of each month (April, May, June). Wahhab's signature animated short-form pieces — stylized, culturally rich visual content designed to stop the scroll and establish brand identity.

**IRL Content:** Shot in the second week of each month. Real-world footage — behind-the-scenes studio sessions, day-in-the-life clips, street culture moments, and merch styling — to humanize the brand and build authenticity.

**Post Content (Ongoing):** From Week 2 onward, 3–5 pieces per week across TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts. Four content pillars: relatable comedy and social commentary, edutainment and cultural quick tips, cultural pride and modern heritage showcases, and day-in-the-life glimpses. All content uses bilingual language mixing (Arabic/English/Franco-Arab).

## Release Window Strategy

The final four weeks before release day (Weeks 7–10) and the weeks after follow a structured escalation strategy inspired by Damien Keyes' Three Cs framework: Context, Consistency, and Community.

### Pre-Release (Weeks 7–10)

**Rally List (Week 6–10):** A curated list of 50–100 people — friends, family, early supporters, street team members, engaged followers — personally messaged and asked to support on release day. They receive early previews and are primed to stream, share, and post on day one.

**Dave Strategy (Week 6–10):** Direct message outreach to engaged followers and commenters. Genuine one-to-one conversations that build personal investment in the release. The goal is to make each person feel like an insider, not a number.

**Ecosystem Cleanup (Weeks 9–10):** All social profiles, bio links, website, and streaming profiles updated and aligned for the release. Old pinned posts replaced, website refreshed with pre-save CTAs, all platforms point to the same cohesive message.

**Content Volume:** 20–30 pieces released in the final two weeks before launch.

### Release Day (June 6, 2026)

**Stories Blitz:** 20–40 Instagram/TikTok Stories throughout the day — countdowns, behind-the-scenes of the release moment, fan reactions, streaming screenshots, street team updates from Egypt.

**Live Streaming:** Wahhab goes live on Instagram and/or TikTok to celebrate with the community in real time.

**Rally List Activation:** Every person on the Rally List receives a personal message with the streaming link and a specific ask (stream, add to playlist, share to Stories).

### Post-Release (Weeks 11–14)

Post-release push (Weeks 11–13) continues with 3–5 posts per week, shifting toward fan reactions, user-generated content reposts, behind-the-scenes stories about the making of the track, and gratitude content. Street team competition continues. Winners announced July 6 (Week 14).

## Public Relations

Earned media opportunities rather than paid promotion. Wahhab will pursue guest appearances and feature placements on established podcasts within the music, culture, and creative industries. Editorial coverage pursued with regional and niche music publications covering Arabic hip-hop, MENA culture, and independent artists. Press outreach begins Weeks 6–7, timed to land coverage around or just before the June 6 release. Target publications: SceneNoise, CairoScene, Rolling Stone MENA.

## Direct Marketing

The official website serves as the primary hub for audience conversion and long-term community building. The platform transitions from merchandise-only to a fully integrated artist hub for brand storytelling, content discovery, and direct communication with fans.

Website optimized to collect email sign-ups via exclusive updates, early access to merch drops, and release announcements. The email list works in tandem with the pre-save campaign: fans who pre-save are directed to the website to join the email list, creating a funnel from casual interest to owned audience. This Rally List becomes the foundation for release-day activation and future campaigns.

Physical brochures and branded stickers distributed through street teams in Egypt. Given June release date and summer vacation timing, distribution focuses on North Coast, popular restaurants, malls, and social gathering spots rather than university campuses.

---

## Guerrilla Marketing

Street team activation is the primary promotional engine. Gamified competition with monetary prize across key locations in Egypt — North Coast, popular restaurants, malls, and social gathering spots during summer months.

Participants earn points by documenting new fan engagement — social media follows, subscriptions, music streams — with higher point values for video testimonials and in-person interactions. Visual proof required for accountability. Street team members receive branded merchandise and sticker sheets; newly recruited fans receive stickers to reinforce brand visibility.

Recruitment intentionally controlled to minimize audience overlap and preserve authenticity. Initial messaging posted once and pinned on Wahhab's social profile, followed by daily short-form "trial reels" released in Arabic using direct, conversational language. These videos intentionally discourage resharing by framing the message as exclusive and personal.

Secondary competition: Sticker Photography Competition (SPG) — creative photography featuring branded stickers placed in public spaces, submissions collected through release day.

## Word-of-Mouth Marketing

Short-form social media content designed to encourage sharing through cultural relevance, conversational tone, and perceived personalization — particularly Arabic-language trial reels that address viewers directly. Street team strategy relies on in-person word-of-mouth: participants incentivized to introduce Wahhab's music through documented fan engagement rather than broad public promotion.

The Damien Keyes–inspired Dave Strategy turns individual DM conversations into personal investment — each person Wahhab reaches out to becomes a node in the word-of-mouth network.

---

## Merchandise Strategy

Merch serves three functions: promotional vehicle, identity reinforcement, and community marker. Every DBLU item in the wild is a walking billboard endorsed by the wearer.

Produced through print-on-demand (POD) model — no upfront inventory investment, rapid design iteration. Exclusivity maintained through design rotation: specific designs available for limited windows before being retired. Street team–exclusive colorways or early-access designs reward the most engaged community members.

Design language: bilingual wordplay, Egyptian pop culture references, Arab diaspora slang, Arabic calligraphy alongside modern streetwear aesthetics. The goal is for fans to wear DBLU as a badge of identity — "this is who I am" — not just a product purchase. Perceived brand localness is tightly linked to identity expressiveness for Egyptian consumers.

## Integration Summary

Organic digital content + selective advertising + earned media publicity + direct marketing + guerrilla activation + Rally List + Dave Strategy + word-of-mouth amplification = consistent messaging across both digital and physical touchpoints. Content drives online interest, street teams convert interest into physical presence, merch turns fans into walking ambassadors, the website captures email sign-ups, and the Rally List activates the entire network on release day.
