# Alaa Farrag — 90-Day Talent Management Roadmap

> [[Wahhab]]'s first talent management client under [[DBLU]]. 90-day roadmap (April–June 2026) to build Alaa Farrag from scratch: infrastructure, content systems, audience growth, training, and first brand deals.

---

## Project Overview

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Client | Alaa Farrag (content creator, based in New Cairo / Sheikh Zayed, Egypt) |
| Manager | Wahhab (DBLU Talent Management, based in Arizona) |
| Platforms | YouTube, Instagram, TikTok |
| Commission | 13% |
| Time Budget | 3–5 hrs/week (of 10–15 hrs total DBLU work) |
| Review Date | July 1, 2026 |

**Brand differentiator (to be finalized in Week 1 identity session):** Cultural bridge between Gulf and Egypt, bilingual content, identity/self-development angles — not just another beauty influencer.

**Content workflow:** Alaa films long-form → Alaa edits → AI clipper generates 30/45/60s shorts → distribute. Alaa uploads everything to a shared Google Drive. Wahhab handles strategy, business, and outreach. Alaa handles content creation, editing, posting, and training.

**Dual-market strategy:** Egypt for building (lower costs, large Arabic audience). UAE/Dubai for monetization (higher CPMs, premium brands). Alaa can move between both.

---

## Starting Position (March 31, 2026)

- TikTok: 11,400 followers
- Instagram: 2,700 followers
- YouTube: < 500 subscribers
- No management contract signed
- No portfolio website or media kit
- Accounts not audited or optimized
- No brand deals or agency relationships
- Budget not discussed with Alaa
- Brand identity / differentiator not defined

## 90-Day Targets (July 1 Review)

- TikTok → 20,000+
- Instagram → 8,000–10,000
- YouTube → 2,000–3,000
- Content: 2+ months consistent posting on all 3 platforms
- Infrastructure: website live, media kit ready, profiles optimized
- Brand deals: 1–2 paid collaborations closed
- Agency apps: submitted to Rekoya, UNN, Base Beauty
- Training: enrolled in at least one on-camera program
- Brand identity: clear differentiator and positioning defined

---

## Month 1: Foundation (April 2026)

Focus: Set up every piece of infrastructure. No outreach yet — just build the machine.

**Week 1 (Apr 1–7) — Business Setup & Identity:** Sign management contract (13% commission, scope, term, exit clause). Budget conversation with Alaa. Brand Identity Session — define differentiator, voice, positioning, content pillars. Audit all social accounts. Set up shared Google Drive. Create content calendar template.

**Week 2 (Apr 8–14) — Profiles & Media Kit:** Optimize all bios/photos/links across platforms. Set up YouTube channel properly. Build media kit v1. Create professional email. Research Cairo Photo Club workshops. Send inquiry to CrEd Academy.

**Week 3 (Apr 15–21) — Content Pipeline Setup:** Finalize April content plan day-by-day. Set up content workflow (film → edit → AI clipper → distribute). Identify and set up AI clipping tool. First batch recorded — minimum 2 YouTube videos. Send inquiries to Luke Lehner and AUC SCE.

**Week 4 (Apr 22–30) — Website & Systems:** Build and launch portfolio website. Set up analytics tracking. Create analytics dashboard. Publish first YouTube video(s). Begin regular posting schedule. Month 1 review.

**Month 1 Milestones:** Contract signed, all profiles optimized, media kit v1 complete, website live, content pipeline operational, brand identity defined, at least 1 training inquiry sent, content calendar active.

---

## Month 2: Ramp Up (May 2026)

Focus: Content flowing consistently. Start outreach to brands and agencies. Enroll in training.

**Week 5 (May 1–7) — Outreach Begins:** Submit applications to Rekoya, UNN Agency, Base Beauty. Create brand pitch template. Ensure consistent posting (4 days/week).

**Week 6 (May 8–14) — Training & Creator Platforms:** Enroll Alaa in first training program (Cairo Photo Club or CrEd). List on creator platforms (Brunch, Collabstr, Influence.co). Direct outreach to 5 small/mid-sized Egyptian brands. Update media kit with real analytics.

**Week 7 (May 15–21) — Content Iteration:** Analyze top-performing content. Adjust strategy based on data. Pitch 3–5 brands. Set up YouTube SEO. Explore YouTube Shorts strategy.

**Week 8 (May 22–31) — Review & Momentum:** Follow up on all agency apps and brand pitches. Mid-project analytics review. Update website with best-performing content. Month 2 review call.

**Month 2 Milestones:** Applications submitted to 3 agencies, enrolled in at least one training program, listed on 2+ creator platforms, outreach to 8–10 brands, 2 months consistent posting, media kit updated, YouTube SEO implemented.

---

## Month 3: Optimization & Revenue (June 2026)

Focus: Close first deals. Analyze everything. Prepare July 1 review with hard numbers.

**Week 9 (Jun 1–7) — Revenue Push:** Apply for YouTube Partner Program (if eligible — 500 subs + 3K watch hours). Set up TikTok monetization. Apply to affiliate programs. Follow up on pending deals. Negotiate and close first brand deal(s).

**Week 10 (Jun 8–14) — Content Scaling:** Evaluate content-to-shorts pipeline efficiency. Plan collaboration content with other MENA creators. Scale posting frequency. Second wave of 5–10 brand outreach.

**Week 11 (Jun 15–21) — Dubai Market Prep:** Research UAE-based brands and agencies. Plan Dubai content shoots. Create UAE-targeted bilingual content. Update media kit with 2.5 months data.

**Week 12 (Jun 22–30) — Review Preparation:** Compile full analytics report. Build review deck (starting position vs current). Document all systems/templates/workflows for reuse. Identify what worked/didn't. Set Q3 goals and strategy. July 1 Review Meeting with Alaa.

**Month 3 Milestones:** 1–2 brand deals closed (or in final negotiation), YouTube monetization applied for, TikTok monetization activated, affiliate programs running, full analytics report compiled, review deck ready, all workflows documented, Q3 goals drafted, UAE market research complete.

## Key Decisions

- **Commission:** 13% of all revenue from deals Wahhab secures or manages
- **Agencies are partners, not competitors:** Rekoya, UNN, etc. provide brand deal access; Wahhab handles career strategy, creative direction, contract oversight
- **Training priority:** (1) Cairo Photo Club, (2) CrEd Academy, (3) Luke Lehner, (4) AUC SCE, (5) AUC MA Acting (dream). Arab Film School ruled out.
- **Content workflow:** Alaa films → edits → uploads to shared Drive. AI clipper generates shorts. Wahhab reviews and provides feedback.
- **Remote management:** Weekly Google Meet check-ins, shared Google Drive, all business development handled remotely from Arizona
